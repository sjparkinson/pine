Small-Leaved Lime

Tilia cordata

The small-leaved lime in fact has quite large leaves! But apparently not as big as the large-leaved one. Or the Common lime. Also strange, the small-leaved lime in fact is the most common lime tree I've seen so far. Yet you don't see ripe limes littering the pavements (many trees planted on city roads are of this species). Maybe all the limes are spirited away by gin-makers? Also, this lime often grows suckers or shoots out of its bole, especially if lower branches have been pruned. It can look a bit messy as a tree.

Did you know?

This particular lime is easily recognised by the way its twigs and new shoots are half-green, half-red.
