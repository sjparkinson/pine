Tulip Tree

Liriodendron tulipifera

Tulip Trees are beautiful and very distinctive, with an unusual leaf shape (see pictures). The flowers are pale green with a bright orange pattern cutting across the bud. Their shape is a bit like a tulip, hence the name.

Tulip Trees are native to eastern North America. They can reach an impressive height of up to 40m. Since they are fast-growing and the wood is robust, they are highly valued for timber and wood carving. Canoes were often carved out of the trunk of Tulip Trees by Native Americans so in the US these trees used to be known as Canoewood trees.Â
