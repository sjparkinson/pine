English Walnut

Juglans regia

The bark is smooth and olive-brown when young, becoming silver-grey with age. The nuts are found in plump green fruits. Shiny, pinnate leaves are grouped into 5-9 paired leaves and a terminal leaf at the end of each stem. Yellow-green catkins.

Did you know?

If you snap English Walnut leaves in half they smell like liquorice.
