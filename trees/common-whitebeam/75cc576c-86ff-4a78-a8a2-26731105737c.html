Common Whitebeam

Sorbus aria (Rosaceae)

Often grows in urban environments (planted there). A native to chalk downlands - fairly small, a big shrub or small tree. In spring, very hairy leaves, making the tree seem almost white from a distance. Watch out, as many (though less common) cousins have a similar fruit.

Did you know?

From a contentious genus! Calling a Whitebeam a Sorbus marks out your allegiances!
